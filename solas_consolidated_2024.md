## CHAPTER I - GENERAL PROVISIONS

### Regulation 1 - Application
The present Convention shall apply to ships entitled to fly the flag of States the Governments of which are Contracting Parties.

### Regulation 2 - Definitions
For the purpose of the present Convention, unless expressly provided otherwise:

**Ship** means a vessel of any type whatsoever operating in the marine environment including non-displacement craft, WIG craft and seaplanes.

**Passenger ship** means a ship which carries more than twelve passengers.

**Cargo ship** means any ship which is not a passenger ship.

**Tanker** means a cargo ship constructed or adapted for the carriage in bulk of liquid cargoes of a flammable nature.

**Fishing vessel** means a vessel used for catching fish, whales, seals, walrus or other living resources of the sea.

**Nuclear ship** means a ship provided with a nuclear power plant.

### Regulation 3 - Exceptions
The present Convention shall not apply to:
- Ships of war and troopships
- Cargo ships of less than 500 gross tonnage
- Ships not propelled by mechanical means
- Wooden ships of primitive build
- Pleasure yachts not engaged in trade
- Fishing vessels

### Regulation 4 - Application
**4.1** Ships constructed on or after 1 January 2024 means ships:
- For which the building contract is placed on or after 1 January 2024; or
- In the absence of a building contract, the keel of which is laid on or after 1 July 2024; or
- The delivery of which is on or after 1 January 2028.

**4.2** Ships constructed before 1 January 2024 shall comply with applicable requirements as specified in each chapter.

### Regulation 5 - Carriage of cargoes
The carriage of cargoes shall be in accordance with the relevant provisions of chapter VI and VII.

## CHAPTER II-1 - CONSTRUCTION - STRUCTURE, SUBDIVISION AND STABILITY, MACHINERY AND ELECTRICAL INSTALLATIONS

### PART A - GENERAL

#### Regulation 1 - Application
**1.1** Unless expressly provided otherwise, this chapter applies to ships the keels of which are laid or which are at a similar stage of construction on or after 1 July 1998.

**1.2** For ships constructed before 1 July 1998, the Administrations shall ensure compliance with applicable requirements.

**1.3** For the purpose of this chapter:
- Ships constructed means ships the keels of which are laid or which are at a similar stage of construction
- Ships constructed on or after 1 January 2024 means ships for which the building contract is placed on or after 1 January 2024, or in the absence of a building contract, the keel is laid on or after 1 July 2024, or delivery is on or after 1 January 2028

#### Regulation 2 - Definitions
**Subdivision length (Ls)** is the greatest projected length of that part of the ship at or below deck or decks limiting the vertical extent of flooding.

**Breadth (B)** is the greatest moulded breadth of the ship at or below the deepest subdivision draught.

**Draught (ds)** is the vertical distance from the moulded base line amidships to the subdivision waterline.

**Deepest subdivision draught** is the waterline which corresponds to the greatest draught.

### PART A-1 - STRUCTURE OF SHIPS

#### Regulation 3-1 - Structure requirements for passenger ships
**3-1.1** The structure of every passenger ship shall be designed to withstand the most severe conditions of service.

**3-1.2** The structural arrangements shall be such as to maintain the watertight and weathertight integrity of the hull.

#### Regulation 3-2 - Structure requirements for cargo ships
**3-2.1** The structure of every cargo ship of 500 gross tonnage and over shall be designed according to recognized standards.

**3-2.2** Structural design shall take into account the operating environment and loading conditions.

#### Regulation 3-8 - Towing and mooring equipment
**3-8.1** Ships shall be provided with arrangements, equipment and fittings of sufficient safe working load to enable the safe conduct of all towing and mooring operations.

**3-8.2** For ships of 3,000 gross tonnage and above constructed on or after 1 January 2024, the mooring arrangement shall be designed based on guidelines developed by the Organization.

**3-8.3** Equipment shall be clearly marked with limitations associated with its safe operation.

### PART B - SUBDIVISION AND STABILITY

#### Regulation 4 - Flooding assumptions
**4.1** The extent of damage for the purpose of subdivision shall be assumed as follows:
- Longitudinal extent: 0.03Ls or 3 m, whichever is less
- Transverse extent: B/5 or 11.5 m, whichever is less
- Vertical extent: from the baseline upwards without limit

**4.2** Where the damage involves transverse bulkheads, the minimum assumed damage shall be increased.

#### Regulation 5 - Permeabilities
**5.1** The permeabilities assumed for spaces shall be as follows:
- Spaces appropriated to stores: 0.60
- Spaces appropriated to accommodation: 0.95
- Spaces appropriated to machinery: 0.85
- Spaces appropriated to liquids: 0 or 0.95

#### Regulation 6 - Passenger ship subdivision and damage stability regulations
**6.1** Every passenger ship shall comply with the subdivision requirements after flooding.

**6.2** The factor of subdivision shall be determined by the length of the ship.

**6.3** A probability of survival equal to or greater than the required subdivision index shall be attained.

### PART B-1 - STABILITY OF PASSENGER SHIPS AND CARGO SHIPS

#### Regulation 7 - Intact stability
**7.1** Every ship shall comply with intact stability criteria.

**7.2** The metacentric height GM shall be not less than 0.15 m.

**7.3** The righting lever GZ shall be at least 0.20 m at an angle of heel equal to or greater than 30°.

#### Regulation 7-2 - Calculation of the factor si
**7-2.1** For passenger ships constructed on or after 1 January 2024, the factor si is taken as zero where the final waterline immerses the lower edge of openings through which progressive flooding may take place.

**7-2.2** The factor si is taken as zero if any vertical escape hatch becomes immersed in any intermediate or final stage of flooding.

#### Regulation 8 - Special requirements for passenger ships
**8.1** Passenger ships shall satisfy specific stability criteria in damaged condition.

**8.2** The ship shall be designed to survive flooding of any adjacent compartments.

#### Regulation 8-1 - Damage stability requirements for passenger ships
**8-1.1** Passenger ships shall attain a subdivision index A not less than the required subdivision index R.

**8-1.2** The attained subdivision index A shall be calculated considering all credible damage cases.

**8-1.3** For ships constructed on or after 1 January 2024, enhanced damage stability support systems may be required.

## CHAPTER II-2 - CONSTRUCTION - FIRE PROTECTION, FIRE DETECTION AND FIRE EXTINCTION

### PART A - GENERAL

#### Regulation 1 - Application
**1.1** This chapter applies to ships constructed on or after 1 July 2002.

**1.2** Ships constructed before that date shall comply with applicable requirements of the 1974 SOLAS Convention.

#### Regulation 2 - Basic principles
**2.1** The basic fire safety objectives are:
- Prevention of fire and explosion
- Reduction of risk to life caused by fire
- Reduction of risk of damage caused by fire
- Containment, control and suppression of fire and explosion
- Provision of adequate and readily accessible means of escape

#### Regulation 3 - Definitions
**Accommodation spaces** include public spaces, corridors, lavatories, cabins, offices, hospitals, cinemas, games and hobby rooms, barber shops, pantries and similar spaces.

**Control stations** include spaces in which ship's radio or main navigating equipment or emergency source of power is located.

**Service spaces** include galleys, pantries, lockers, mail and specie rooms, storerooms, workshops and similar spaces.

### PART B - MEASURES FOR PREVENTION OF FIRE AND EXPLOSION

#### Regulation 4 - Probability of ignition
**4.1** The risk of ignition of flammable cargo vapours shall be minimized.

**4.2** Potential sources of ignition shall be controlled.

#### Regulation 5 - Fire growth potential
**5.1** Materials and structural arrangements shall be such as to minimize fire growth potential.

**5.2** Primary deck coverings shall have low flame-spread characteristics.

### PART C - SUPPRESSION OF FIRE

#### Regulation 10 - Fixed fire extinguishing systems
**10.1** Fixed fire extinguishing systems shall be provided where required.

**10.2** Systems shall be capable of manual and automatic release.

**10.3** Carbon dioxide systems shall not be used in accommodation spaces.

#### Regulation 11 - Automatic sprinkler, fire detection and fire alarm systems
**11.1** Passenger ships shall be fitted with an automatic sprinkler system.

**11.2** Cargo ships of 500 gross tonnage and over shall have fire detection systems.

## CHAPTER III - LIFE-SAVING APPLIANCES AND ARRANGEMENTS

### PART A - GENERAL

#### Regulation 1 - Application
**1.1** This chapter applies to ships constructed on or after 1 July 1998.

**1.2** Ships shall carry life-saving appliances which comply with requirements of this chapter.

#### Regulation 2 - Definitions
**Life-saving appliance** means a lifeboat, liferaft, rescue boat or other approved appliance.

**Survival craft** means a craft capable of sustaining the lives of persons in distress.

#### Regulation 3 - Evaluation, testing and approval
**3.1** Life-saving appliances shall be approved by the Administration.

**3.2** Appliances shall be tested according to the recommendations of the Organization.

### PART B - REQUIREMENTS FOR SHIPS

#### Regulation 6 - Communications
**6.1** Ships shall be provided with means for making distress alerts.

**6.2** Two-way radio communication shall be available.

#### Regulation 7 - Personal life-saving appliances
**7.1** Every person on board shall be provided with a lifejacket.

**7.2** Immersion suits shall be provided where required.

**7.3** Thermal protective aids shall be available.

#### Regulation 21 - Survival craft and rescue boats
**21.1** Passenger ships shall carry sufficient survival craft to accommodate all persons on board.

**21.2** Cargo ships shall carry survival craft for at least the total number of persons on board.

## CHAPTER IV - RADIOCOMMUNICATIONS

### PART A - GENERAL

#### Regulation 1 - Application
**1.1** This chapter applies to ships engaged on international voyages.

**1.2** Ships shall carry radio equipment complying with this chapter.

#### Regulation 2 - Terms and definitions
**GMDSS** means the Global Maritime Distress and Safety System.

**Sea areas** are defined as:
- Sea area A1: VHF coast station range
- Sea area A2: MF coast station range  
- Sea area A3: INMARSAT geostationary satellite coverage
- Sea area A4: other sea areas

### PART B - MAINTENANCE REQUIREMENTS

#### Regulation 15 - Maintenance requirements
**15.1** Equipment shall be so maintained as to ensure availability of functional requirements.

**15.2** Methods to ensure availability include duplication, shore-based maintenance, or at-sea electronic maintenance.

### PART C - RADIO EQUIPMENT CARRIAGE REQUIREMENTS

#### Regulation 7 - Radio equipment - General
**7.1** Ships shall carry radio equipment for distress and safety communications.

**7.2** Equipment shall be capable of transmitting ship-to-shore, ship-to-ship, and search and rescue distress alerts.

**7.3** For ships operating in sea area A1, VHF DSC and survival craft VHF radio are required.

**7.4** Ships in sea area A3 shall carry equipment for continuous alerting through recognized mobile satellite service (RMSS).

#### Regulation 11 - Radio equipment - Cargo ships
**11.1** Cargo ships shall carry:
- VHF radio installation
- MF/HF radio installation for areas A2, A3, A4
- INMARSAT or IRIDIUM ship earth station for area A3
- VHF watch receiver
- Search and rescue radar transponder
- Two-way VHF radiotelephone apparatus

**11.2** Equipment shall enable compliance with distress and safety communication requirements.

## CHAPTER V - SAFETY OF NAVIGATION

### Regulation 1 - Application
**1.1** This chapter applies to all ships on all voyages except warships, troopships, and ships solely navigating the Great Lakes.

### Regulation 2 - Definitions
**ECDIS** means Electronic Chart Display and Information System.

**AIS** means Automatic Identification System.

### Regulation 15 - Principles relating to bridge design
**15.1** The bridge shall be designed to facilitate safe navigation.

**15.2** Equipment arrangement shall allow effective bridge team operation.

### Regulation 19 - Carriage requirements for shipborne navigational systems
**19.1** Ships shall carry navigational equipment as specified.

**19.2** ECDIS is required for ships constructed on or after 1 July 2012.

**19.3** AIS shall be fitted on ships of 300 gross tonnage and upwards.

### Regulation 20 - Voyage data recorders
**20.1** Passenger ships and cargo ships of 3000 gross tonnage and upwards constructed on or after 1 July 2002 shall be fitted with a voyage data recorder (VDR).

**20.2** VDR shall store information concerning the position, movement, physical status, command and control of the vessel.

## CHAPTER VI - CARRIAGE OF CARGOES

### Regulation 1 - Application
**1.1** This chapter applies to the carriage of cargoes (except liquids in bulk covered by chapter VII).

### Regulation 2 - Cargo information
**2.1** The shipper shall provide the master or his representative with cargo information.

**2.2** Information shall include description, gross mass, special properties, and stowage and handling precautions.

### Regulation 5 - Stowage and securing
**5.1** Cargo and cargo units shall be loaded, stowed and secured throughout the voyage.

**5.2** Cargo securing arrangements shall comply with the Cargo Securing Manual.

### Regulation 7 - Cargo Securing Manual
**7.1** Ships carrying cargo shall have a Cargo Securing Manual approved by the Administration.

**7.2** The manual shall include information on securing arrangements and cargo securing devices.

## CHAPTER VII - CARRIAGE OF DANGEROUS GOODS

### PART A - CARRIAGE OF DANGEROUS GOODS IN PACKAGED FORM

#### Regulation 1 - Definitions
**Dangerous goods** include substances, materials and articles covered by the IMDG Code.

**IMDG Code** means the International Maritime Dangerous Goods Code.

#### Regulation 3 - Requirements for the carriage of dangerous goods
**3.1** Dangerous goods shall be carried in accordance with the IMDG Code.

**3.2** Ships carrying dangerous goods shall comply with construction and equipment requirements.

### PART A-1 - CARRIAGE OF DANGEROUS GOODS IN SOLID FORM IN BULK

#### Regulation 6 - Application
**6.1** This part applies to ships carrying dangerous goods in solid form in bulk.

#### Regulation 7 - Requirements
**7.1** Carriage shall be in accordance with the IMSBC Code.

**7.2** Ships shall comply with structural and equipment requirements.

### PART B - CONSTRUCTION AND EQUIPMENT OF SHIPS CARRYING DANGEROUS LIQUID CHEMICALS IN BULK

#### Regulation 8 - Application
**8.1** This part applies to ships carrying dangerous liquid chemicals in bulk.

#### Regulation 12 - Requirements
**12.1** Ships shall comply with the IBC Code.

**12.2** Construction, systems, and equipment shall meet code requirements.

### PART C - CONSTRUCTION AND EQUIPMENT OF SHIPS CARRYING LIQUEFIED GASES IN BULK

#### Regulation 15 - Application
**15.1** This part applies to ships carrying liquefied gases in bulk.

#### Regulation 17 - Requirements
**17.1** Ships shall comply with the IGC Code.

**17.2** All aspects of design and operation shall conform to code provisions.

## CHAPTER VIII - NUCLEAR SHIPS

### Regulation 1 - Application
**1.1** This chapter applies to nuclear ships.

### Regulation 2 - Application of other chapters
**2.1** Nuclear ships shall comply with other applicable chapters of this Convention.

### Regulation 3 - Exemption
**3.1** A nuclear ship may be exempted from other requirements provided equivalent safety is achieved.

### Regulation 4 - Nuclear ship safety assessment
**4.1** A nuclear ship safety assessment shall be submitted to the Administration.

### Regulation 9 - Operating manual
**9.1** Every nuclear ship shall have an operating manual approved by the Administration.

## CHAPTER IX - MANAGEMENT FOR THE SAFE OPERATION OF SHIPS

### Regulation 1 - Definitions
**ISM Code** means the International Safety Management Code.

**Company** means the owner of the ship or any other organization which has assumed responsibility for operation.

### Regulation 2 - Application
**2.1** This chapter applies to passenger ships and cargo ships of 500 gross tonnage and upwards.

### Regulation 3 - Safety management objectives
**3.1** The objectives are to ensure safety at sea and prevention of pollution.

### Regulation 4 - Safety management requirements
**4.1** Ships shall be operated by companies holding a Document of Compliance.

**4.2** Ships shall carry a Safety Management Certificate.

### Regulation 6 - Certification
**6.1** A Document of Compliance shall be issued to companies complying with the ISM Code.

**6.2** A Safety Management Certificate shall be issued to ships.

## CHAPTER X - SAFETY MEASURES FOR HIGH-SPEED CRAFT

### Regulation 1 - Definitions
**High-speed craft** means a craft capable of maximum speed equal to or exceeding 3.7∇^0.1667 m/s.

### Regulation 2 - Application
**2.1** This chapter applies to high-speed craft of 500 gross tonnage and upwards on international voyages.

### Regulation 3 - Requirements for high-speed craft
**3.1** High-speed craft shall comply with the HSC Code.

**3.2** Construction and equipment shall meet enhanced safety standards.

## CHAPTER XI-1 - SPECIAL MEASURES TO ENHANCE MARITIME SAFETY

### Regulation 1 - Authorization of recognized organizations
**1.1** Recognized organizations acting on behalf of the Administration shall be authorized.

### Regulation 3 - Ship identification number
**3.1** Ships shall be issued a ship identification number in accordance with the IMO scheme.

### Regulation 4 - Enhanced surveys

### Regulation 5 - Continuous synopsis record
**5.1** Ships shall carry a continuous synopsis record providing information on the ship's history.

## CHAPTER XI-2 - SPECIAL MEASURES TO ENHANCE MARITIME SECURITY

### Regulation 1 - Definitions
**ISPS Code** means the International Ship and Port Facility Security Code.

**Ship security** means measures to protect ship and port facilities against security incidents.

### Regulation 2 - Application
**2.1** This chapter applies to passenger ships and cargo ships of 500 gross tonnage and upwards.

### Regulation 4 - Requirements for ships
**4.1** Ships shall comply with the ISPS Code.

**4.2** Ships shall carry an International Ship Security Certificate.

### Regulation 6 - Ship security alert system
**6.1** Ships shall be provided with a ship security alert system.

**6.2** The system shall transmit ship-to-shore security alerts.

## CHAPTER XII - ADDITIONAL SAFETY MEASURES FOR BULK CARRIERS

### Regulation 1 - Definitions
**Bulk carrier** means a ship constructed to carry dry cargo in bulk.

### Regulation 2 - Application
**2.1** This chapter applies to bulk carriers of 500 gross tonnage and upwards.

### Regulation 4 - Structural requirements
**4.1** Bulk carriers shall meet enhanced structural standards.

### Regulation 6 - Water ingress alarms
**6.1** Bulk carriers shall be fitted with water level detectors in cargo holds.

### Regulation 12 - Loading and unloading requirements
**12.1** Loading and unloading shall be conducted safely according to approved procedures.

## AMENDMENTS AND UPDATES

### 2024 Amendments
- MSC.474(102) - Construction and stability requirements
- MSC.482(103) - Water level detectors for cargo ships
- MSC.496(105) - Radio communications (GMDSS modernization)

### Key Changes in 2024:
1. Enhanced requirements for ships constructed on or after 1 January 2024
2. Updated GMDSS requirements including IRIDIUM satellite services
3. Improved towing and mooring equipment standards
4. Enhanced damage stability calculations
5. Water level detection systems for multiple hold cargo ships

## IMPLEMENTATION NOTES

**Entry into Force:** The 2024 amendments entered into force on 1 January 2024.

**Transitional Arrangements:** Ships constructed before 1 January 2024 continue to comply with applicable previous requirements unless specifically required to upgrade.

**Flag State Implementation:** Flag States are required to ensure ships flying their flag comply with applicable requirements.

**Port State Control:** Port States may verify compliance during inspections.
